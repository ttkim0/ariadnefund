Working papers from the Ariadne Labs research team.

The papers below are the same documents we share with prospective limited partners and counterparty due-diligence teams. Each paper is reproducible end-to-end from public NOAA, NWS, and Kalshi data plus the code in our repository. We publish so that the system is auditable; we publish in arxiv preprint format because that is the register in which our work is most legible.

Papers

    Probabilistic Forecasting and Trading on Event Markets: A Case Study of Daily Temperature Contracts at San Francisco International Airport

    Terry Kim, Andrew Liang, Krit Phaisamran · Stanford University

    May 2026 · 33 pp · 65 refs · 12 figs · PDF (415 KB)

  2. 02

    NWP-Conditioned Daily-Extreme Models: Closing the 12–48h Skill Gap

    Kim, Liang, Phaisamran · in preparation

    Forthcoming. Adds GFS, HRRR, and ECMWF outputs as features to the daily-extreme quantile models, expected to extend the high-skill regime from $\le12$h to $\le48$h.

  3. 03

    Multi-Station Event Markets: Capacity, Correlation, and Capital Allocation

    Kim, Liang, Phaisamran · in preparation

    Forthcoming. Replicates the architecture across LAX, NYC, BOS, ORD, MIA, PHX and characterizes capacity and cross-station return correlation.

  4. 04

    A Contextual-Bandit Position-Sizing Layer for Calibrated Probability Forecasts

    Kim, Liang, Phaisamran · in preparation

    Forthcoming. Replaces fixed fractional-Kelly sizing with Thompson sampling on a Beta-Bernoulli contextual bandit indexed by edge, hours-to-settle, spread, open interest, and strike type.

Reproducibility

All numbers in Paper 01 are reproducible from the source data and the fourteen Python scripts in our repository. The pipeline runs end-to-end on a single laptop in approximately twenty-five minutes, excluding the optional thirty-minute training of the daily-extreme models. All trained model weights total under seven megabytes; all parquet outputs total under two hundred megabytes. External dependencies are limited to pandas, scikit-learn, pyarrow, and scipy. No proprietary data source, no cloud GPU, no closed-source library is used in any step.
